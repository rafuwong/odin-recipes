Yangzhou Fried Rice

Description

Yangzhou Fried Rice is a classic Chinese dish known for its colorful ingredients and savory flavor. It typically includes shrimp, ham, peas, and carrots, all stir-fried with rice and seasoned with soy sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup diced ham
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the shrimp and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of oil and saut√© the diced ham, peas, and carrots for about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble until fully cooked.
  5. Add the cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps. Stir everything together.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce and mix well. Add the cooked shrimp back into the skillet.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently.
  8. Garnish with chopped green onions if desired and serve hot.
