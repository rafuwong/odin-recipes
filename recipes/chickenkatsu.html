Chicken Katsu

Description

Chicken Katsu is a popular Japanese dish consisting of breaded and deep-fried chicken cutlets. It is typically served with a side of shredded cabbage and a tangy katsu sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Katsu sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Shredded cabbage for serving
  • Cooked rice for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oil in a deep skillet or pot to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Dredge each chicken breast in flour, shaking off excess.
  4. Dip the floured chicken into the beaten eggs, allowing excess to drip off.
  5. Coat the chicken with panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere.
  6. Carefully place the breaded chicken into the hot oil and fry for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Remove the chicken from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  8. Slice the chicken katsu into strips and serve with katsu sauce, shredded cabbage, and rice if desired.
  9. Enjoy your homemade Chicken Katsu!
